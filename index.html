Simple Taiwanese Popcorn Chicken Recipe (鹹酥雞)

A famous Taiwanese night market snack. These crispy chicken bites are marinated in a savory mix, deep-fried to perfection, and finished with fresh Thai basil leaves for a fragrant crunch.

Preparation time

  • Total: Approximately 30 minutes
  • Preparation: 20 minutes (including marinating)
  • Cooking: 10 minutes

Ingredients

  • 500g boneless chicken thighs (cut into bite-size pieces)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice wine (or mirin)
  • 1 tsp five-spice powder
  • ½ tsp white pepper
  • 1 clove garlic (minced)
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 egg (optional, helps binding)
  • ½ cup sweet potato starch (or cornstarch for coating)
  • Fresh Thai basil leaves (for garnish)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a bowl, combine soy sauce, rice wine, five-spice powder, pepper, garlic, ginger, and sugar. Add the chicken and mix well. Let marinate for at least 20 minutes.
  2. Optional egg mix: Add a beaten egg to the marinated chicken for extra coating stickiness.
  3. Coat the chicken: Toss the chicken pieces in sweet potato starch until evenly coated.
  4. Heat the oil: In a deep pan, heat oil to around 170–180°C (340–355°F).
  5. Fry the chicken: Fry chicken in batches for 3–4 minutes until golden and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  6. Fry basil (optional but traditional): Quickly fry Thai basil leaves for a few seconds until crispy be careful, they splatter!
  7. Serve: Top the chicken with fried basil and a sprinkle of salt and white pepper. Serve hot!
